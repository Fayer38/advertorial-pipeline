A Heartfelt Story That Went Viral

For over 15 years, I’ve spent my days in a small, cozy workshop creating delicate, one-of-a-kind jewelry alongside my daughter. Each piece took time, care, and attention, and gifted to close friends and family. But now, after many years of creating together, we reached a moment we never expected. Large mass-production companies have made it increasingly difficult for small family studios like ours to keep going.

 

“It breaks my heart,” I often say with a warm smile. “Big companies can sell jewelry for less than what it costs us to create. I’d rather see our pieces worn and loved than hidden away while mass-produced items take over.”

 

For a long time, our jewelry remained a quiet secret.

Then my daughter shared our story online, and something unexpected happened — Hundreds of women fell in love with our designs.

More Than Jewelry: A Legacy of Handcrafted Beauty

Our jewelry isn’t just about style — it’s about care, detail, and legacy. Each piece is created with time and attention, and no two ever feel exactly the same. For over 15 years, my daughter and I have poured our hearts and experience into designs that feel timeless and elegant, yet deeply personal.

 

Every ring, necklace, and earring carries a story. From choosing each stone to the final finishing touches, every detail is considered to bring out the unique beauty of each design. Wearing one of our pieces means carrying a small part of our life’s work — a true treasure.

 

“I want our jewelry to be more than just something you wear,” I often say. “It should carry memories and emotions.”

 

Women are falling in love with our designs because they aren’t just accessories—they’re heirlooms that will be cherished for generations.

Some of my last pieces.

A Defining Moment — A Chance to Keep Our Dream Alive

After 15 years of creating jewellery side by side with my daughter, we’ve reached a defining moment.

This collection represents a chance to see whether our small family studio can continue and keep our dream alive.

 

This sale is our way of seeing whether, together, we can stand up to mass production and keep this way of creating jewellery alive — so that we can continue designing meaningful pieces for women not just today, but for many years to come.

 

As part of this Revival Sale, selected pieces are now available with reductions of up to 50%.

With this, we hope to reach as many women as possible who value meaningful designs — and who don’t feel connected to mass-produced jewelry. Many women have already chosen to support us, and I’m deeply grateful.

With hundreds of pieces already finding new homes, only a limited number of designs remain.

 

This moment matters more than we ever expected.

Every piece chosen helps us understand whether this journey can continue — and whether there is still room for jewelry created with care and meaning in today’s world.
