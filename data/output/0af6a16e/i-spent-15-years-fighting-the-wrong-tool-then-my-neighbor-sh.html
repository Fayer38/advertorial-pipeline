The Blower That Was Supposed to Make My Life Easier

My name is David. I'm 58, I own a house in the suburbs, and for the past 12 years I've had a gas-powered leaf blower sitting in my garage.

It cost me $220. It weighs 11 pounds. And every single fall, I'd dread the moment I had to drag it out.

I bought it because that's what homeowners do, right? You get a real blower. A serious one. Something with enough power to actually move leaves.

Except here's the thing no one tells you: a tool you dread using is a tool that doesn't work.

The Hidden Cost of the 'Real' Tool

Every autumn it was the same ritual. Pull the blower out from the back of the garage. Check if there's gas. There never was. Drive to the gas station. Mix the fuel. Try to start it — three pulls, five pulls, eight pulls.

By the time it was running, my back was already tight.

Then 45 minutes of noise, exhaust fumes, and vibration that would make my hands tingle for hours. And at the end of it? A clean patio. But also a wrecked Saturday morning and two days of back pain.

I'd tried other things too. A rake — fine for leaves, useless for everything else. A broom — same problem. An electric blower with a cable — I tripped over it twice and nearly went through the garage window.

What I hadn't tried was admitting that maybe the problem wasn't my effort. Maybe it was the tool itself.
