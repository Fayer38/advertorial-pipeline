The Morning I Almost Asked My Son for Help

I've owned my home for 31 years. Big oak trees, half-acre lot, a driveway that collects every leaf in the neighborhood come October. For most of those years, I'd knock the whole property out in an afternoon without giving it a second thought.

Then my late fifties arrived. The old gas blower I'd had since 2004 — loud, heavy, stubborn — started feeling less like a tool and more like a punishment. I'd haul it out, yank the cord four or five times, and spend an hour hunched forward fighting it with both hands. By Sunday evening, I was done for the weekend.

I wasn't tired of the work. I was tired of what the work cost me the next morning.

My son started offering to drive over on weekends to help. I always said no. Politely, but firmly. Because the day I can't blow leaves off my own driveway is the day something important changes about who I am.
