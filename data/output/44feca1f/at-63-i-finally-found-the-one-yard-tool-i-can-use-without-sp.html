The Dirty Secret About Gas Blowers Nobody Talks About After 55

Here’s what nobody tells you when you buy a gas blower: it was engineered for a 35-year-old landscaper who uses it professionally, eight hours a day.

It was never designed for you.

A standard gas blower weighs between 8 and 12 pounds — before you fill the tank. The starter cord requires a sharp, sudden pull of 15 to 20 pounds of force. The vibration travels straight up your arm into your shoulder and neck. After 20 minutes, your back knows it happened.

And if it doesn’t start on the first pull? You pull again. And again. Until something gives — usually your patience, your rotator cuff, or both.

Warning: The repetitive yanking motion of a gas blower starter cord is one of the leading causes of rotator cuff strain in adults over 50 — an injury that often requires months of physical therapy and can permanently limit shoulder mobility.
