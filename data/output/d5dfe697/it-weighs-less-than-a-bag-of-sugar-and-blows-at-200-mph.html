Why Most Blowers Stay in the Garage

Nobody talks about this, but it's real: the problem with most blowers isn't performance — it's inertia.

You look at the patio. There are leaves. You think about going to get the blower. You do the mental math: find it, move the stuff in front of it, check the fuel, deal with the choke, pull the cord, wait for it to warm up. For a 10-minute job? You grab a broom instead. Or you leave the leaves there.

And if you've got any back issues — or you're just not 35 anymore — carrying a heavy machine around for 20 minutes isn't just inconvenient. It hurts.

I've talked to plenty of people who bought a gas blower, used it twice, and never looked back. Not because it didn't work. Because the overhead wasn't worth it.

Meanwhile, the patio stays dirty. The car air-dries with water spots. The frost stays on the windshield until you're already late. Small problems that pile up because the solution is too much trouble.
