What the $400 Hardware Industry Doesn't Want You to Know

Here's the uncomfortable truth our investigation surfaced: the outdoor power tool industry has almost no incentive to build lighter products.

Heavy gas blowers require fuel. Fuel means repeat purchases. They require spark plug replacements, air filter changes, carburetor cleaning every season. Every service visit is revenue. Every broken part is a sale.

A brushless cordless blower with a lifetime warranty and zero maintenance requirements? That's a business problem for the traditional hardware model — not a priority.

It's the same reason major appliance brands resisted efficient motors for decades. Not because the technology wasn't available. Because the incentive to change wasn't there.

Meanwhile, homeowners over 55 — the largest property-owning demographic in the country — have quietly been shelving their rakes, calling their adult children, or paying for services they used to handle in 15 minutes on a Saturday morning.
