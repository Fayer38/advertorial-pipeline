When Your Tools Are Working Against You

Here's what nobody talks about: the tools designed to make yard work easier were built for someone 30 years younger with a strong back and two free arms.

A standard gas blower weighs between 10 and 15 pounds. You haul it out of the garage, check the fuel, yank the starter cord three or four times — please, not your shoulder again — hoist it up, and wrestle it around your property for 25 minutes. By the time you're done, your back is aching, your arms are done, and you need a rest before you can even put it away.

And that's on a good day.

Warning: Pulling a gas blower starter cord is one of the most common causes of rotator cuff injury in adults over 55. Most people don't realize the damage is happening until it's too late.

So the blower stays in the garage. You use a rake — slower, harder. Or you ask for help, which feels like giving something up. Or you watch the leaves pile up and tell yourself you'll deal with it next weekend.

None of those are real solutions. And deep down, you know it.
