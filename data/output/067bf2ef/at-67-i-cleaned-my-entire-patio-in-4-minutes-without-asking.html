The Tools We Grew Up With Were Never Built for Us

Here's something nobody talks about: the average gas-powered leaf blower weighs between 10 and 23 pounds. Add a full fuel tank and you've got something that would tire out a 30-year-old's arms after a few minutes.

For those of us in our 50s, 60s, or 70s — people who are still active, still capable, still want to do things ourselves — these tools don't just cause fatigue. They cause real, lasting injury.

Warning: The #1 cause of back and shoulder injuries among homeowners 55+ isn't lifting furniture or shoveling snow — it's repetitive strain from yard tools. Heavy blowers, jerky starter cords, and forced two-handed grips put enormous stress on joints that are already working harder than they used to.

The starter cord alone is responsible for a significant number of rotator cuff injuries every single year in adults over 55. That sharp, awkward yanking motion was simply never designed for our bodies.

But here's what really gets me: we keep using these tools not because they work well for us — but because we assume that if you want real power, you need real weight. We accept the soreness as the price of staying independent.
