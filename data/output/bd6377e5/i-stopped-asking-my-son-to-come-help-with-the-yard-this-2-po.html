Yanking the cord four times to start it. Holding it at shoulder height for 45 minutes while the engine vibrated up through my arms.

I'd finish and need two Advil and a long sit-down before I could do anything else that day.

That's not complaining. That's just honest. A body at 64 is not a body at 40. And the tools designed for 40-year-olds haven't changed.

The Dirty Secret About Outdoor Power Tools

Here's what nobody tells you when you walk into the hardware store:

Most outdoor power tools were designed decades ago for professional landscapers — young, working in crews, handing the equipment off after a few hours. They were never built for one person, working alone, in their 60s.

And yet every fall, millions of homeowners haul out that same 10-pound gas blower, yank the cord until their shoulder burns, breathe exhaust for 45 minutes, and then wonder why they're sore for three days afterward.

Warning: The average gas-powered blower weighs between 8 and 12 pounds. Add sustained vibration, overhead arm positioning, and 30 to 45 minutes of repetitive motion — and you have a well-documented recipe for back strain, shoulder injury, and wrist fatigue. For adults over 55, strenuous yard work is one of the most common triggers for back problems that lead to lasting mobility issues.

And here's what makes it worse: the obvious alternative — just stopping — isn't really an alternative at all. This is your home. These are your standards. You're not ready to hand that over to someone else.
