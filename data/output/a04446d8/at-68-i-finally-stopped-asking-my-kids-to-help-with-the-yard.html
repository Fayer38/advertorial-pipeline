A Personal Message From Daniel, Founder of Seese

My name is Daniel. I built the Seese Pro because of my father.

Five years ago, Dad turned 72. He'd always been the kind of man who took care of everything himself — the lawn, the gutters, the driveway. He never asked for help and was proud of it. Then one October morning, I got a call.

He'd tried to start his gas blower. The pull cord snapped back hard. He lost his balance on the wet driveway. Three cracked ribs and a bruised shoulder.

The ER doctor told me it happens more than most people realize. A heavy, off-balance tool. A slippery morning. One wrong movement — and everything changes.

I spent the next three years designing something different. Something a 72-year-old man could hold in one hand without straining, powerful enough to replace every tool in the shed, and simple enough that there's nothing to pull, nothing to maintain, and nothing that could hurt you.

That became the Seese Pro.

Now here's the part I wish I didn't have to tell you.

We completed our first major production run last fall. Fifteen thousand units, stored in a warehouse in Tennessee. I was ready. Excited. Then, two months ago, I got another call — this time from my co-founder and closest friend of over two decades.

His wife had been diagnosed with stage 3 cancer. He needed to step away immediately. Our financing agreement required him to remain active in the business. The bank called the loan.

I have until the end of next month to move the entire inventory.

That's why, for a limited time, I'm offering every unit at 55% off — not as a marketing trick, but because I need to convert inventory into cash, honor what I owe, and make sure every Seese Pro ends up in the hands of someone who genuinely needs it.

The Tools That Were Built for a Younger Version of You

Think about the last time you used your gas blower.

You carried it out. Checked the gas. Pulled the cord — once, twice, maybe three times. Felt the weight shift into your wrist, your elbow, your lower back. And by the time you were done, you weren't just tired from the yard work. You were tired from the tool.

That's not weakness. That's physics. A standard gas blower weighs 20 to 23 pounds. It was designed in an era when nobody was thinking about a 68-year-old homeowner using it solo on a Saturday morning.

Warning: Outdoor power tools are among the leading causes of back and shoulder injuries in adults over 55. Most incidents involve repeated pull-start motions or carrying heavy equipment on uneven or wet terrain.

And here's what nobody says out loud: it's not the yard that wears you down. It's the quiet feeling that you're losing ground. That the things you used to handle without thinking are slowly, one by one, getting handed off.

The rake takes too long and destroys your joints. The corded blower turns your patio into an obstacle course. And asking the kids? That's not a solution — that's a surrender.
